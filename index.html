💡 Did You Know?

In the late 1800s, during the construction of the Brooklyn Bridge, a seemingly insurmountable problem arose. The builders needed a way to transport massive amounts of rock debris from the foundation of the bridge. Enter Emily Warren Roebling, the wife of the chief engineer. When her husband fell ill, Emily stepped in, mastering engineering principles and overseeing the completion of the bridge—becoming the first woman to play such a significant role in a major construction project. This remarkable tale highlights the unheralded contributions of women in historically male-dominated fields and underscores the power of determination and innovation in shaping our world.
