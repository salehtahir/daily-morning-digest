💡 Did You Know?

In 1957, aboard the Sputnik 2 spacecraft, the first living creature to orbit the Earth was not a human but a dog named Laika. This little stray dog from Moscow captured the hearts of people worldwide with her journey into space. Laika's story sheds light on the sacrifices made in the early days of space exploration and reminds us of the ethical considerations that have evolved in scientific advancements. Her legacy serves as a poignant reminder of the bond between humans and animals in our quest for knowledge beyond our planet.
