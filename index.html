Social Philosophy: The Veil of Ignorance
The concept of the "Veil of Ignorance" was introduced by the philosopher John Rawls in his book "A Theory of Justice." Rawls suggests that in order to create a fair and just society, we should make decisions behind a hypothetical veil where we are ignorant of our own identity, social status, wealth, talents, etc. This ensures that decisions are made impartially and without bias, as individuals would not know how their choices might personally benefit or harm them.
