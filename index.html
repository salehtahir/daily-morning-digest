🧠 Philosophical Insight

**Social Philosophy** **Social Contract Theory** Social Contract Theory is a cornerstone of social philosophy that suggests individuals consent to the rules and norms of society in exchange for protection and order. This idea, proposed by thinkers like Thomas Hobbes, John Locke, and Jean-Jacques Rousseau, delves into the notion of a hypothetical agreement people make with each other to form a society. It raises fundamental questions about the nature of obligations between citizens and their government, as well as the legitimacy of authority. **
