Social Philosophy: Solidarity
Solidarity is a core principle in social philosophy that emphasizes individuals binding together in shared values, goals, or struggles, fostering unity and mutual support within a community. It is the recognition that we are interconnected and share a responsibility towards one another's well-being and common good. Solidarity challenges individualism and encourages cooperative efforts that transcend personal interests for the betterment of society as a whole.
How can we cultivate a sense of solidarity in an increasingly polarized world where division and self-interest often dominate social dynamics?
