💡 Did You Know?

In 1770, a young Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart was commissioned to write a funeral piece in honor of his mentor, Archduke Maximilian Franz of Austria. Devastated by the Archduke's passing, Mozart poured his grief into composing the intricate and moving "Masonic Funeral Music." This somber masterpiece not only showcases Mozart's profound emotional depth but also highlights the deep connections between the world of music and the secretive rituals of the Freemasons, shedding light on the intersecting spheres of art and society in the 18th century. It's a poignant reminder that even the most celebrated composers grappled with personal loss and sought solace in their craft.
