Epistemology: Foundationalism vs. Coherentism
Foundationalism and coherentism are two contrasting theories within epistemology that attempt to explain how beliefs are justified. Foundationalism argues that there are basic beliefs that serve as the foundation for all other beliefs, while coherentism suggests that the justification for any belief comes from its coherence with a system of beliefs. This debate raises fundamental questions about the structure of knowledge and the nature of rational justification.
