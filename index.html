🧠 Philosophical Insight

Metaphysics: The Problem of Universals The problem of universals in metaphysics deals with the question of the existence and nature of qualities, attributes, or properties that multiple things have in common. One side argues that universals exist independently of particular instances (realism), while the other side believes they are simply names put on shared characteristics (nominalism). This debate has profound implications for our understanding of the nature of reality and how we categorize our experiences.
