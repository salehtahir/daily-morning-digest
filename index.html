üí° Did You Know?

Have you ever heard of the Japanese art of kintsugi, where broken pottery is repaired with gold or silver lacquer? Well, the origins of this beautiful practice can be traced back to a 15th-century shogun named Ashikaga Yoshimasa, who sent a damaged Chinese tea bowl back to be fixed with unsightly metal staples. The craftsman who mended the bowl found a more aesthetic solution by using precious metals, inadvertently creating a tradition that celebrates imperfection and resilience. This tradition reminds us that sometimes, our flaws can be transformed into something unique and even more valuable, reflecting a profound lesson in embracing imperfections in both objects and ourselves.
