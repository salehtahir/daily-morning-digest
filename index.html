🧠 Philosophical Insight

Social Philosophy: The Veil of Ignorance
The concept of the Veil of Ignorance, introduced by the philosopher John Rawls, proposes that in determining principles of justice, individuals should imagine themselves behind a hypothetical "veil" that obscures their own personal characteristics. This thought experiment prompts people to make decisions impartially, as they wouldn't know their social status, abilities, gender, or any other personal traits that could bias their judgment. It emphasizes the importance of fairness and the consideration of different perspectives in designing just societies.
How would the world change if more of our social and political decisions were made with the idea of the Veil of Ignorance in mind?
