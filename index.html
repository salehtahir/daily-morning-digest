Epistemological Fallibilism
Epistemological fallibilism is the view that no belief can be certain and infallible; all knowledge is contingent and subject to revision in light of new evidence or arguments. This concept challenges the idea of absolute certainty in one's beliefs, highlighting the importance of intellectual humility and openness to new perspectives. Embracing fallibilism reminds us to approach knowledge with a critical eye, recognizing the inherent limitations of human understanding.
How does accepting the fallibility of our beliefs affect our willingness to engage in meaningful dialogue and consider alternative viewpoints?
