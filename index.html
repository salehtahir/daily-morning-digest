Social Philosophy: The Veil of Ignorance
The "veil of ignorance" is a concept introduced by philosopher John Rawls, suggesting that in designing a fair society or system, individuals should make decisions without knowing their own place in society, their social class, or personal characteristics. This thought experiment aims to encourage impartiality and fairness by forcing people to consider the needs and rights of all individuals, regardless of their own circumstances. By applying the veil of ignorance, we can strive towards a more just and inclusive society.
How would society change if everyone made decisions without knowledge of their own identities or advantages?
