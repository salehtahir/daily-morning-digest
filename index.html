💡 Did You Know?

In 1869, a young chemist named Constantin Fahlberg made a shocking discovery during a routine lab experiment. Focused on coal tar derivatives, he absentmindedly forgot to wash his hands before eating dinner that evening. To his surprise, the bread he ate tasted incredibly sweet. This accidental taste test led to the creation of saccharin, the first artificial sweetener, revolutionizing the food industry and forever changing how we perceive sweetness. Fahlberg's serendipitous moment not only sparked a new era in food science but also highlighted the unexpected ways in which innovation can arise from everyday mishaps.
