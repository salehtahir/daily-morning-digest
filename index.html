💡 Did You Know?

In the early 1900s, a lesser-known figure named Henrietta Lacks unknowingly made a pivotal contribution to medical history. Henrietta's cells, taken without her consent during a routine procedure, turned out to be the first immortal human cell line, revolutionizing biomedical research. Her cells, known as HeLa cells, have been vital in numerous scientific breakthroughs, highlighting the often-unseen human stories behind groundbreaking discoveries and the importance of informed consent in medical research. Henrietta Lacks' legacy prompts us to reflect on the complex intersection of ethics, science, and human rights in the advancement of modern medicine.
