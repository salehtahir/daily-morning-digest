Social Philosophy: The Veil of Ignorance
The concept of the "Veil of Ignorance" was introduced by the philosopher John Rawls in his theory of justice. Rawls suggests that in designing a just society, individuals should make decisions without knowing their own place in that society. This way, people would make fairer choices that benefit everyone, not just themselves.
To what extent do my choices and actions consider the interests and well-being of others, especially those who may be in less advantageous positions than myself?
