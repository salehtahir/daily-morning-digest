Epistemology: Concept of A Priori Knowledge
A priori knowledge refers to knowledge that is independent of experience. According to philosophers like Immanuel Kant, certain truths are known purely through reason and do not rely on empirical evidence. This contrasts with a posteriori knowledge, which is derived from experience. Understanding the distinction between a priori and a posteriori knowledge helps us contemplate the nature of knowledge acquisition and the limitations of sensory perception.
