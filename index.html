Epistemology: Gettier Problem
The Gettier problem is a classic challenge in epistemology that questions the traditional definition of knowledge. It presents scenarios where someone can have a justified true belief that something is true, yet that belief is not considered knowledge because it relies on luck or coincidence rather than genuine understanding. This dilemma forces us to rethink what it truly means to know something and to explore the limitations of our current definitions of knowledge.
How comfortable are you with the idea that what you believe to be true might not actually constitute knowledge?
